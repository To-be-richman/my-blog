**Core Consensus: The honeymoon period of the policy transition has been choked off by geopolitical oil prices, and the alarm for liquidity tightening has sounded again. The volatility at Wall Street's historic highs is not the endgame; the surge in US Treasury yields and the storm in overseas bond markets are becoming a garrote for high-valuation assets.**

### 1. News
*   **Summit Lacks Details, Fueling Energy Supply Anxiety**: The US-China bilateral summit in Beijing concluded. Despite the optimistic official tone, the lack of substantive trade deliverables caused market optimism to recede quickly. Meanwhile, with the Strait of Hormuz remaining blockaded, international oil prices surged pre-market (WTI jumped 3.4% to $104.55), pushing market fears of H2 "stagflation" to a climax [Reuters].
*   **Fed "Warsh Era" Begins, Hawk Expectations Land**: New Fed Chair Kevin Warsh officially took the oath of office today. Confronted with PPI data showing the largest increase in four years and a deeply divided FOMC, the market widely expects Warsh to maintain interest rates "higher for longer." The 10-year US Treasury yield surged to a one-year high of 4.55% in response [Investopedia, Reuters].
*   **UK Bond Crash Becomes a Black Swan, Triggering Global Resonance**: As Prime Minister Starmer faces a leadership challenge within his party, market fears of a widening fiscal deficit caused the UK 10-year Gilt yield to skyrocket to 5.14%, a new high since 2008 [The Wall Street Journal]. The collective rise in sovereign bond yields is directly draining liquidity from the equity market [The Wall Street Journal].

### 2. Market Outlook
*   **Heavy Profit-Taking at Highs**: Dragged down by a comprehensive pre-market decline in the three major stock index futures (Nasdaq futures plunged 1.3%), the S&P 500 and the Dow face strong profit-taking pressure at today's open after hitting historic highs in the previous session [Reuters].
*   **Extreme Sector Divergence**: Growth tech giants led by Nvidia (NVDA) and Tesla (TSLA), along with high-valuation semiconductor sectors, are highly sensitive to interest rates and will lead the decline today. The aviation sector is weakening across the board due to soaring fuel costs. Only defensive commodities (energy stocks) and specific restructuring plays (such as Dexcom) are expected to see safe-haven capital inflows against the trend [Reuters].

Lock in the 10-year US Treasury yield, which is currently at a one-year high, to hedge against macro uncertainties and serve as the ultimate moat against any liquidity stampede.
*   **30% Safe-Haven & Alternative Assets (Gold / Crypto)**: Overweight inflation-resistant hard assets. Use gold to combat credit contraction, while employing Bitcoin (oscillating near the $80,500 high) as an asymmetric liquidity hedge in a high-inflation, fiat-debasement environment.
*   **20% Energy & Commodities (Hard Asset Hedge)**: Overweight crude oil and commodity targets driven by geopolitical and supply chain constraints to directly offset the valuation erosion brought by rising inflation on traditional financial assets.
