Global financial markets closed the week on Friday with an extremely dramatic divergence. Despite consumer sentiment plunging to historic lows due to soaring gas prices, US equities staged a powerful weekend rally [The Street, Motley Fool].

The Dow Jones Industrial Average surged 294 points, forcefully striking a **new historic high** at 50,579.70; the S&P 500 secured its eighth consecutive weekly gain, marking its **longest winning streak** since late 2023 [CNBC, MarketWatch].

Yet, the underlying logic of this rally isn't flawless economic health, but rather capital repositioning and hedging amid macroeconomic uncertainty.

### 1. News Flash: New Fed Chair Sworn In & Diplomatic Smokescreen in US-Iran Talks
*   **White House Ceremony, Warsh Takes the Helm**: Kevin Warsh was officially sworn in as Fed Chairman at the White House on Friday, becoming the first central bank chief since Alan Greenspan in 1987 to take the oath at the White House [CNBC]. Though Trump emphasized Fed independence, this unconventional ceremony indicates a shifting policy playbook [CNBC].
*   **US-Iran Progress Pulls Oil Down From Highs**: Secretary of State Blinken and Senator Rubio hinted at "progress" in peace talks [The Street, Barron's], triggering a **5.24% weekly plunge** in Brent crude to $102.58/bbl, with WTI settling at $96.35 [The Street, Barron's]. The short-term cooling of geopolitical inflation prepped the ground for Friday's bond rebound [CNBC, The Street].
*   **Rate Expectations Drastically Reshuffled**: While falling oil pulled the 10-year US Treasury yield back slightly to 4.55% [CNBC], the Fed's hawkish minutes have fully erased rate-cut expectations for the year, sending the **implied probability of a rate hike soaring to 80%** [AMP].

### 2. Outlook: Structural Divergence Behind the Euphoria
*   **Tech and Consumer Giants Polarize**: In a high-interest-rate environment, capital is accelerating into absolute monopolies holding vast free cash flows. Qualcomm spiked 18% this week on positive partnership news [CNBC], while Dell and HP jumped double digits ahead of earnings as institutions front-ran AI server demand [Motley Fool].
*   **Retail Sentiment Collapses, Correction Risks Mount**: High fueling costs and interest rates are physically biting into real-world consumer spending, prompting Goldman Sachs to issue a clear warning [Motley Fool]: The decoupling between record-high stock indexes and near-record-low consumer confidence is severely stretched. Prolonged yields and sticky inflation are significantly raising the risk of a **moderate technical correction** this summer [Motley Fool].

### 3. Strategy: Adjusting the Defensive Matrix Ahead of Memorial Day
With US markets closed on Monday (May 25) for Memorial Day [WSJ], investors have a rare window for strategic rebalancing. Use this pause to execute the following:
*   **Trim Speculative Valuation Foams**: High interest rates will eventually starve edge-AI concepts lacking positive free cash flow. Utilize Friday's broad bounce to unconditionally cut or reduce exposure to unprofitable tech and recent IPOs [Motley Fool].
*   **Anchor the Risk-Free Frontier**: Capitalize on the 10-year Treasury yield's brief dip to 4.55% [The Street] to lock a portion of realized gains into 3-6 month short-term Treasuries or USD cash [AMP]. Cash in a high-yield era isn't trash; it's the strongest umbrella.
*   **Maintain Hard Asset Strategic Allocation**: Despite oil's 5.5% weekly drop [The Street], defensive supply chain front-loading will persist as long as the Strait of Hormuz remains a friction point [AMP, CaixaBank]. Maintain a 10%~20% weight in gold and physical commodities as terminal insurance against h2 stagflation [Sina Finance, AMP].
