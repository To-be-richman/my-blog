## Introduction

Japan may finally be approaching a historic monetary transition.

For decades, the Bank of Japan maintained ultra-loose monetary policy, suppressing yields and supporting liquidity across global markets.

Now, rising inflation and wage growth are forcing the BOJ to reconsider its framework.

## Yield Curve Pressure

The Japanese bond market remains under pressure as long-term yields continue rising.

Global investors are closely monitoring the sustainability of Japan's debt structure.

Any major policy shift could impact:

- USDJPY
- Global liquidity
- Japanese equities
- US Treasury demand

## Impact on Global Markets

A stronger yen could create volatility across risk assets.

Technology and semiconductor sectors may also experience short-term positioning changes due to shifts in global capital flows.

## Final Thoughts

Liquidity remains the core driver of modern markets.

Understanding central bank policy is no longer optional for global investors.
